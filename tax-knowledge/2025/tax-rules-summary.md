# Tax Year 2025 - Key Rules and Instructions

## Filing Requirements

### Who Must File

| Filing Status | Age | Gross Income Threshold |
|---------------|-----|------------------------|
| Single | Under 65 | $15,000 |
| Single | 65 or older | $17,000 |
| Married Filing Jointly | Both under 65 | $30,000 |
| Married Filing Jointly | One 65+ | $31,600 |
| Married Filing Jointly | Both 65+ | $33,200 |
| Married Filing Separately | Any age | $5 |
| Head of Household | Under 65 | $22,500 |
| Head of Household | 65 or older | $24,500 |
| Qualifying Surviving Spouse | Under 65 | $30,000 |
| Qualifying Surviving Spouse | 65 or older | $31,600 |

### Filing Deadlines
- **Regular deadline:** April 15, 2026
- **With extension:** October 15, 2026
- **Sinployees living abroad:** June 15, 2026 (automatic 2-month extension)

## Standard Deduction vs. Itemizing

### Standard Deduction Amounts (2025)
- **Single:** $15,000
- **Married Filing Jointly:** $30,000
- **Married Filing Separately:** $15,000
- **Head of Household:** $22,500
- **Additional for 65+/Blind (Single):** $2,000 each
- **Additional for 65+/Blind (Married):** $1,600 each

### When to Itemize
Itemize if your total of:
- State/local taxes (up to $10,000)
- Mortgage interest
- Charitable contributions
- Medical expenses (above 7.5% of AGI)
- Other eligible deductions

**exceeds your standard deduction.**

## Income Categories

### Wages and Compensation (Line 1)
- W-2 wages (Box 1)
- Tips not reported to employer
- Dependent care benefits (taxable portion)
- Household employee wages

### Interest Income (Line 2)
- **Line 2a:** Tax-exempt interest (municipal bonds) - not taxed but reported
- **Line 2b:** Taxable interest (savings, CDs, Treasury bonds)

### Dividend Income (Line 3)
- **Line 3a:** Qualified dividends (taxed at capital gains rates)
- **Line 3b:** Ordinary dividends (taxed as ordinary income)

### Retirement Distributions (Lines 4-5)
- **IRA distributions:** Report total (4a) and taxable portion (4b)
- **Pensions/annuities:** Report total (5a) and taxable portion (5b)

### Social Security Benefits (Line 6)
- Report total benefits (6a)
- Calculate taxable portion (6b) - up to 85% may be taxable based on provisional income

### Capital Gains (Line 7)
- From Schedule D
- Long-term gains taxed at 0%, 15%, or 20% depending on income
- Short-term gains taxed as ordinary income

## Key Deductions and Adjustments

### Above-the-Line Deductions (Schedule 1)
These reduce AGI and are available even if you don't itemize:
- Educator expenses (up to $300)
- HSA contributions
- Self-employment tax (50%)
- Self-employed health insurance
- IRA contributions (traditional)
- Student loan interest (up to $2,500)
- Alimony paid (pre-2019 agreements only)

### Itemized Deductions (Schedule A)

#### Medical Expenses
- Only amounts exceeding 7.5% of AGI
- Includes: doctor visits, prescriptions, insurance premiums, dental, vision
- Does NOT include: cosmetic surgery, gym memberships, vitamins

#### State and Local Taxes (SALT)
- **Cap: $10,000** ($5,000 if Married Filing Separately)
- Includes: state income OR sales tax + property taxes
- Cannot exceed cap regardless of actual amount paid

#### Mortgage Interest
- On acquisition debt up to $750,000
- On debt incurred before Dec 15, 2017: up to $1,000,000
- Home equity loan interest: only if used for home improvements

#### Charitable Contributions
- Cash: up to 60% of AGI
- Appreciated property: up to 30% of AGI
- Must have written acknowledgment for gifts $250+
- Must have qualified appraisal for property $5,000+

## Tax Credits

### Child Tax Credit
- **Amount:** $2,000 per qualifying child under 17
- **Refundable portion:** Up to $1,700 (Additional Child Tax Credit)
- **Phaseout begins:** $200,000 single / $400,000 married

### Earned Income Tax Credit (EITC)
| Children | Max Credit | Income Limit (Single) | Income Limit (Married) |
|----------|------------|----------------------|------------------------|
| 0 | $649 | $19,104 | $26,214 |
| 1 | $4,328 | $50,434 | $57,554 |
| 2 | $7,152 | $57,310 | $64,430 |
| 3+ | $8,046 | $61,555 | $68,675 |

### Child and Dependent Care Credit
- Up to $3,000 of expenses for one dependent
- Up to $6,000 for two or more dependents
- Credit rate: 20% to 35% based on income

### Education Credits
- **American Opportunity Credit:** Up to $2,500/student (first 4 years)
- **Lifetime Learning Credit:** Up to $2,000/return

## Self-Employment

### Self-Employment Tax
- **Rate:** 15.3% (12.4% Social Security + 2.9% Medicare)
- **Social Security wage base:** $176,100
- **Deduction:** 50% of SE tax is deductible from income

### Quarterly Estimated Taxes
- Due: April 15, June 15, September 15, January 15
- Must pay 100% of prior year tax OR 90% of current year tax to avoid penalty
- 110% of prior year if AGI > $150,000

### Qualified Business Income (QBI) Deduction
- **Deduction:** 20% of qualified business income
- **Phaseout begins:** $197,300 (single) / $394,600 (married)
- Subject to W-2 wage and capital limitations above threshold

## Retirement Contributions

### 401(k) / 403(b) / 457
- **Limit:** $23,500
- **Catch-up (50+):** +$7,500
- **Catch-up (60-63):** +$11,250

### IRA (Traditional and Roth)
- **Limit:** $7,000
- **Catch-up (50+):** +$1,000
- Traditional IRA deduction phases out if covered by workplace plan

### SEP IRA
- **Limit:** 25% of compensation, up to $70,000

### SIMPLE IRA
- **Limit:** $16,500
- **Catch-up (50+):** +$3,500

---

## Health Savings Accounts (HSA)

### Contribution Limits
- **Self-only:** $4,300
- **Family:** $8,550
- **Catch-up (55+):** +$1,000

### Requirements
- Must have High Deductible Health Plan (HDHP)
- HDHP minimum deductible: $1,650 (self) / $3,300 (family)
- HDHP max out-of-pocket: $8,300 (self) / $16,600 (family)

---

## Capital Gains Tax Rates

### Long-Term (held > 1 year)

| Rate | Single | Married Filing Jointly |
|------|--------|------------------------|
| 0% | $0 - $48,350 | $0 - $96,700 |
| 15% | $48,351 - $533,400 | $96,701 - $600,050 |
| 20% | $533,401+ | $600,051+ |

### Net Investment Income Tax (NIIT)
- **Rate:** 3.8%
- **Applies to:** Investment income above $200,000 (single) / $250,000 (married)

## Recordkeeping Requirements

### How Long to Keep Records
- **3 years:** Most tax returns and supporting documents
- **6 years:** If you underreported income by >25%
- **7 years:** If you claimed a loss from worthless securities
- **Indefinitely:** If you filed fraudulent return or didn't file

### Required Documentation
- W-2s, 1099s, 1098s
- Receipts for deductions over $75
- Written acknowledgment for charitable gifts $250+
- Qualified appraisal for property donations $5,000+
- Basis records for investments (until sold + 3 years)
