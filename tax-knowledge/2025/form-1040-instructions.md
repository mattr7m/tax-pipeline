## Filing Requirements

### Who Must File

You must file a return if any of the following apply:

| Filing Status | Under 65 | 65 or Older |
|--------------|----------|-------------|
| Single | $15,000 | $17,000 |
| Married Filing Jointly | $30,000 (both under 65) | $31,600 (one 65+) |
| Married Filing Separately | $5 | $5 |
| Head of Household | $22,500 | $24,500 |
| Qualifying Surviving Spouse | $30,000 | $31,600 |

**You must also file if:**
- You owe any special taxes (AMT, household employment taxes, etc.)
- You received HSA, Archer MSA, or Medicare Advantage MSA distributions
- You had net earnings from self-employment of $400 or more
- You received advance payments of the premium tax credit

---

## Line 1a: Wages, Salaries, Tips

**What to enter:** Total wages, salaries, tips from Form(s) W-2, box 1

**Include:**
- Wages, salaries, bonuses, commissions
- Tips reported to employer
- Taxable fringe benefits
- Sick pay (if taxable)
- Dependent care benefits over $5,000

**Do NOT include:**
- Employer contributions to 401(k) (already excluded from W-2 box 1)
- Employer HSA contributions
- Tax-exempt combat pay (report on line 1i instead)

**Special cases:**
- Multiple W-2s: Add all box 1 amounts together
- Corrected W-2 (W-2c): Use the corrected amounts
- Missing W-2: Contact employer; if not received by February, use Form 4852

**References:** Publication 17, Publication 525

---

## Line 1b: Household Employee Wages Not on W-2

**What to enter:** Wages paid to you as a household employee if employer didn't provide W-2

**When this applies:**
- Employer paid you less than $2,800 in 2025 (W-2 not required)
- You worked as babysitter, housekeeper, caretaker, etc.

**Documentation:** Keep records of payments received

---

## Line 1c: Tip Income Not Reported on Line 1a

**What to enter:** Tips not reported to your employer

**Include:**
- Cash tips not reported to employer
- Allocated tips shown in W-2 box 8 (unless you have records of actual tips)
- Non-cash tips (fair market value)

**Special rule:** If you received $20 or more in tips in any month, you should have reported them to your employer

**References:** Publication 531 (Reporting Tip Income)

---

## Line 1d: Medicaid Waiver Payments

**What to enter:** Medicaid waiver payments from Form(s) W-2 or Form 1099

**Include:**
- Difficulty of care payments for caring for someone in your home
- Both taxable and nontaxable amounts (if choosing to include for EITC)

**Election available:** 
- These payments are generally excludable from income
- You may elect to include them as earned income to claim EITC
- If excluding, also enter on Schedule 1, line 8s

**References:** Notice 2014-7

---

## Line 2a: Tax-Exempt Interest

**What to enter:** Interest that is exempt from federal income tax

**Include:**
- Interest from municipal bonds
- Exempt-interest dividends from mutual funds (Form 1099-DIV, box 12)

**Do NOT include:**
- Interest from your IRA, HSA, or education savings accounts
- This is informational only - not added to taxable income

**Note:** Some tax-exempt interest may be subject to AMT (private activity bonds)

---

## Line 2b: Taxable Interest

**What to enter:** Total taxable interest income

**Include:**
- Bank account interest
- CD interest
- Treasury bond interest (exempt from state tax, but federally taxable)
- Savings bond interest (unless using for education)
- Original issue discount (OID)

**Schedule B required if:**
- Total exceeds $1,500, OR
- You received interest as nominee, OR
- Other conditions listed in Schedule B instructions

**References:** Schedule B instructions, Publication 550

---

## Line 3a: Qualified Dividends

**What to enter:** Dividends eligible for lower tax rates (from 1099-DIV, box 1b)

**Requirements for qualified status:**
- Held stock for more than 60 days during 121-day period around ex-dividend date
- From domestic corporation or qualified foreign corporation

**Tax rates on qualified dividends:**
- 0% if taxable income below $48,350 (single) / $96,700 (MFJ)
- 15% for income up to $533,400 (single) / $600,050 (MFJ)
- 20% above those thresholds

---

## Line 3b: Ordinary Dividends

**What to enter:** Total ordinary dividends (from 1099-DIV, box 1a)

**Note:** This amount includes qualified dividends - line 3a is a subset of line 3b

**Schedule B required if:**
- Total exceeds $1,500, OR
- You received dividends as nominee

---

## Lines 4a-4b: IRA Distributions

**Line 4a:** Total IRA distributions received (from 1099-R, box 1)
**Line 4b:** Taxable amount

**Fully taxable if:**
- All contributions were deductible (traditional IRA)
- No nondeductible contributions ever made

**Partially taxable if:**
- You made nondeductible contributions (use Form 8606)

**Not taxable (do not include) if:**
- Qualified Roth IRA distribution (5-year rule met, age 59½+)
- Trustee-to-trustee transfer (not reported as distribution)

**Rollovers:**
- Check box 1 on line 4c if rolled over
- Enter full amount on 4a, taxable amount on 4b
- 60-day rollover rule applies

**Special Situations:**
- Qualified Charitable Distribution (QCD): Check box 2, enter "QCD" - up to $105,000 excludable
- Required Minimum Distribution (RMD): Must take if age 73+

**References:** Publication 590-B

---

## Lines 5a-5b: Pensions and Annuities

**Line 5a:** Total pension/annuity payments (from 1099-R, box 1)
**Line 5b:** Taxable amount (from 1099-R, box 2a, or calculate using Simplified Method)

**Fully taxable if:**
- You did not contribute to the plan (employer-funded)
- You already recovered your full cost

**Use Simplified Method Worksheet if:**
- Annuity starting date after November 18, 1996
- Payments from qualified plan
- You contributed to the cost

**Retired Public Safety Officers:**
- May exclude up to $3,000 for health/LTC insurance premiums
- Distribution must be from eligible retirement plan

**References:** Publication 575, Publication 721

---

## Lines 6a-6b: Social Security Benefits

**Line 6a:** Total Social Security benefits (from SSA-1099, box 5)
**Line 6b:** Taxable amount (0% to 85% may be taxable)

**Calculating taxable amount:**
Use Social Security Benefits Worksheet in instructions:

1. Up to 50% taxable if provisional income exceeds:
   - $25,000 (single)
   - $32,000 (MFJ)

2. Up to 85% taxable if provisional income exceeds:
   - $34,000 (single)
   - $44,000 (MFJ)

**Provisional income =** AGI + nontaxable interest + 50% of Social Security benefits

**References:** Publication 915

---

## Line 7: Capital Gain or (Loss)

**What to enter:** Net capital gain or loss from Schedule D, line 21 (or line 16 if not required to file Schedule D)

**When Schedule D required:**
- You have capital gain distributions
- You have gains or losses from sales of capital assets
- Form 1099-B received

**When Schedule D NOT required:**
- Only capital gain distributions (no other gains/losses)
- No Form 1099-B received
- Can report directly on line 7

**Loss limitation:** Deductible loss limited to $3,000 per year ($1,500 MFS)

**References:** Schedule D instructions, Publication 550

---

## Line 12: Standard Deduction OR Itemized Deductions

**Standard Deduction Amounts (2025):**
| Filing Status | Amount |
|--------------|--------|
| Single | $15,000 |
| MFJ / QSS | $30,000 |
| MFS | $15,000 |
| HOH | $22,500 |

**Additional amounts for 65+ or blind:**
- Single/HOH: $2,000 each
- MFJ/MFS: $1,600 each

**Must itemize if:**
- You are MFS and spouse itemizes
- You are a nonresident alien
- You file a return for less than 12 months due to accounting period change

**Consider itemizing if total of:**
- State/local taxes (up to $10,000)
- Mortgage interest
- Charitable contributions
- Medical expenses (above 7.5% AGI)
- **exceeds your standard deduction**

**References:** Schedule A instructions

---

## Line 16: Tax

**Calculate using one of:**
1. Tax Table (income under $100,000)
2. Tax Computation Worksheet (income $100,000+)
3. Qualified Dividends and Capital Gain Tax Worksheet (if line 3a or line 7 > 0)
4. Schedule D Tax Worksheet (if required)

**Order of worksheets:**
- Use Schedule D worksheet if required
- Otherwise use Qualified Dividends worksheet if applicable
- Otherwise use Tax Table or Tax Computation Worksheet

---

## Line 25: Federal Income Tax Withheld

**Line 25a:** From Form(s) W-2, box 2
**Line 25b:** From Form(s) 1099 (various boxes)
**Line 25c:** Other withholding (W-2G, 1042-S, etc.)
**Line 25d:** Total (add 25a + 25b + 25c)

**Attach:** Forms W-2 to your return (if filing by mail)

---

## Line 34: Overpayment / Line 37: Amount You Owe

**If line 33 > line 24:** You overpaid - enter difference on line 34
- Choose refund (line 35a) and/or apply to next year (line 36)

**If line 24 > line 33:** You owe - enter difference on line 37
- Pay by April 15 to avoid interest and penalties
- Options: Direct Pay, EFTPS, credit/debit card, check
